What is a Ceiling Fan?

It is an appliance that is designed with a motor and blades that rotate to help keep a room cool. The fans are set up on the ceiling where they can move the air around and reduce the heat in a particular space. Although the ceiling fan may be considered a tad outdated compared to the cooling technology that homeowners have today, it remains a useful device. The ability of ceiling fans to achieve a cooling effect without consuming a lot of power is what keeps them relevant. Ceiling fans are available in a wide range that this review will look at in a bid to narrow down the best options for users.

How Do Ceiling Fans Work?

Ceiling fans look like simple designs from the outside, but a lot goes on to achieve the cooling effect. One vital element to point out in this review is that ceiling fans don’t actually cool the air, meaning that they don’t do anything to change the temperature of the air.

Advantages and Applications

This review helps homeowners get the best from ceiling fans. A ceiling fan is best for cooling during the hot season, but it has its benefits in the cold season as well. By altering the settings on ceiling fans, you can maintain some degree of warmth during winter. For this reason, it is best to get a fan that allows you to change the orientation of the blades.

A Few Facts about Ceiling Fans

Ceiling fans go back a long way. They can be traced to 500 BC India where punkah fans were used to move the air around to cool it. The rotary fan made its first appearance in the 1860s where a water-run turbine was responsible for turning the blades. This model of the ceiling fan gained popularity because it could house several fan units. Commercial spaces such as restaurants, stores and offices invested in it.

Hugger Ceiling Fans

Also called low-profile, this ceiling fan is built for low ceilings. Hugger fans are best used on ceilings that are less than 8 feet from the floor. The fans are designed to mount flush on the ceiling directly on the bracket. This installation ensures that the fan clears the mandatory distance of 7 feet from the floor. A low-profile fan would also be the best choice for a vaulted ceiling. A ceiling adapter may be necessary to angle the fan appropriately.
